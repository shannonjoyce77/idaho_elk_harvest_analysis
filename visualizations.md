## Visual 1: Total Elk Harvested 2001-2024
*Line Graph*

🔗 **[View Interactive Version on Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/shannon.joyce5716/vizzes)**


**What it Shows:**
This line graph tracks total elk harvested across Idaho each year from 2001 to 2024. Harvest numbers start low, under 10,000 annually in the early 2000’s, before spiking to its highest point in 2005 with 16,647 elk harvested. From there it dips and stabilizes around 10,000 through 2013, then climbs again with another spike in 2014 and 2015 (12,886–15,048). From 2015 onward harvest rates settle into a consistent range in the low to mid 13,000s.

**Key Takeaway** 
Elk harvest rates have largely stabilized over time, with less dramatic fluctuation compared to the early 2000s. This plateau suggests more consistent herd dynamics, likely reflecting effective long-term wildlife management practices.

## Visual 2: Where Are Idaho's Elk Being Harvested?
*Interactive Map of Idaho*

🔗 **[View Interactive Version on Tableau Public](https://public.tableau.com/app/profile/shannon.joyce5716/vizzes)**


**What it Shows:**
This interactive map of Idaho breaks total elk harvest down by hunting unit collectively from 2001-2024. Warm tone colors indicate higher harvest totals and cool tones indicate lower harvest levels. Hovering over any unit reveals the unit number and total elk harvested. The map shows that the majority of elk harvesting occurs in northern Idaho, with Region 4 leading at 16,333 total elk harvested, followed by Region 10A at 14,944. 

One interesting outlier is Hunting Unit 39. Region 39 is located in lower central Idaho near Boise and Mountain Home, it posts a strong harvest total of 12,441 despite being further south than the other top performers. This is likely driven by its proximity to a large population center, indicating  high hunter participation in that unit. The bottom left corner of the state consistently shows the lowest harvest totals.

**Key Takeaway:**
Northern Idaho dominates elk harvest totals, likely due to larger herd sizes and habitat conditions such as National Forest land and high elevation. Harvest success strongly correlates with habitat type and hunter participation.
